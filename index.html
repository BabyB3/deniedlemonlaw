What is Denied Lemon Law?

Denied Lemon Law is a consumer service of SecondLook for California drivers whose lemon-law claim was denied.

It organizes your repair orders, service notes, messages, and denial letter into a single timeline and shows whether the documentation reveals a recurring defect pattern — the kind of pattern that is easy to miss when records are scattered across multiple repair visits.

Common reasons lemon law claims get denied

Most denials come down to a handful of reasons — and several are more answerable than they look. Each has a free guide on how to respond:

  • Denied for "not enough repair attempts"
  • Denied because the dealer "could not duplicate" the problem
  • Denied because of mileage
  • Denied — repairs "not covered under warranty"
  • Denied over modifications or aftermarket parts

Is Denied Lemon Law a law firm?

No. Denied Lemon Law and its parent company SecondLook are a vehicle-records analysis service. The assessment is informational only and does not provide legal advice or representation.

My claim was already denied — does that mean it's over?

Not necessarily. Many denials turn on documentation that was incomplete, or on details that were never clearly connected across visits. Denied Lemon Law focuses on whether your repair records and the wording in your denial letter actually tell the full story.

Is this related to criminal-justice "second look" or resentencing?

No. Denied Lemon Law and SecondLook work only on vehicle defects and California lemon law. They are unrelated to criminal sentencing, resentencing, or post-conviction "second look" programs.

How does Denied Lemon Law relate to My Lemon Check and Case Clarity?

All three are consumer services of SecondLook. Denied Lemon Law serves drivers whose claims were already denied, My Lemon Check is the lemon-law screening tool, and Case Clarity is the assessment portal.
